Strategic Data Center Exit

$55k/yr Savings

Executive Summary

Faced with an imminent colocation lease expiration and aging hardware liabilities, I orchestrated a complete exit to Azure. This was not just a technical "lift and shift" but a strategic "move and improve" program that eliminated technical debt, established a governed cloud foundation, and transitioned the organization to an OpEx financial model.

Business Context & Constraints

  • Compelling Event: Lease expiry in 6 months with severe financial penalties for holdovers.
  • Financial Pressure: CapEx budget frozen; mandate to shift to consumption-based OpEx.
  • Skill Gap: Internal Ops team deep in VMware but zero Azure experience.
  • Regulatory: Strict data residency and encryption mandates (Healthcare/FinTech alignment).

Architecture & Strategy

1. The "Hub-Spoke" Topology

Designed a scalable network architecture using Azure Enterprise Scale (CAF) principles:

  • Hub VNet: Centralized connectivity (ExpressRoute Gateway) and security (Azure Firewall).
  • Identity Subnet: Extended Active Directory to Azure for seamless authentication.
  • Spoke VNets: Workload isolation for Prod and Non-Prod environments.

2. "Pilot-First" Migration Strategy

To mitigate risk, I rejected a "Big Bang" cutover. We adopted a wave-based approach:

  • Wave 0 (Foundation): Landing Zone deployment and security baseline validation.
  • Wave 1 (Pilot): Low-risk internal tools to validate network/DNS and build team confidence.
  • Wave 2 (Production): Mission-critical apps migrated over a weekend cutover window.

Ownership & Governance

My Role (Principal Architect):

  • Owned the High-Level Design (HLD) and Security Strategy.
  • Led the Technical Review Board (TRB) for wave approval.
  • Managed stakeholder communications (C-Level updates on TCO and Timeline).
  • Defined the "Definition of Ready" for workload migration.

The Delivery Team:

  • Network Engineers executed firewall rule implementations.
  • SysAdmins managed the Azure Migrate appliance and replication agents.

Outcomes & Impact

Financial

Realized 30% TCO reduction by utilizing Azure Hybrid Benefit and Right-Sizing instances during migration.

Operational

Reduced provisioning time from weeks to hours via Terraform automation. Established 99.99% uptime SLA.
